# Amazon-Fresh-Analytics-with-SQL

## 🔹 Project Overview

Designed and analyzed a relational dataset covering customers, orders, products, suppliers, and revenue

Built a structured database to support efficient data storage and analysis

Focused on customer behavior, product performance, and supplier insights

Delivered insights to improve inventory, customer engagement, and revenue growth

## 🔹 Tools & Technologies Used

SQL → Database design, queries, joins, aggregations, and constraints

Microsoft Excel → Data cleaning and preprocessing

## 🔹 Key Features

Customer analysis including top spenders and inactive users

Product performance analysis based on revenue, ratings, and stock levels

Supplier analysis for inventory and product availability

Revenue analysis across cities and product categories

Customer satisfaction analysis using product ratings

## 🔹 Data Analysis Performed

Identified top spending customers and inactive users

Analyzed Prime membership distribution across cities

Evaluated product performance by revenue and ratings

Detected low-stock products and supplier dependencies

Analyzed revenue trends by city and product categories

Performed customer satisfaction analysis using ratings data

## 🔹 Dashboard Insights

### Customer Insights

Few cities have multiple Prime users; most cities have limited adoption

Some customers remain inactive without placing orders

### Product Analysis

Top categories drive major revenue

High-rated products (>4) indicate strong customer preference

Low-stock products identified for replenishment

### Supplier Insights

Certain suppliers dominate product availability

Supplier dependency impacts stock availability

### Revenue Insights

Revenue concentrated in specific cities and categories

High-performing categories contribute most to sales

### Customer Satisfaction

Top-rated products drive engagement

Low-rated products highlight improvement areas

## 🔹 Key Business Insights

Presence of inactive customers affecting revenue potential

Strong dependency on top-performing product categories

Inventory challenges due to low-stock products

Uneven Prime membership distribution across cities

Supplier concentration creates operational risks

## 🔹 Business Recommendations

Re-engage inactive customers with personalized offers

Promote high-rated products to increase conversion rates

Improve inventory management through stock alerts and forecasting

Expand Prime membership through city-specific campaigns

Diversify suppliers to reduce dependency risks

Bundle products and optimize pricing to increase average order value

## 🔹 Project Outcome

Improved understanding of customer behavior and product performance

Enabled data-driven inventory and supplier management decisions

Identified opportunities for revenue growth and customer retention

Built a scalable analytics system for business performance tracking
